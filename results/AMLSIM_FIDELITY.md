# AMLSim fidelity check — Cupel substrate vs. real AMLSim output

> Generated by `tools/amlsim_fidelity.py` ($0, no model calls). Reference is IBM AMLSim's **real Java/MASON sample output**, vendored verbatim in `data/reference/amlsim_sample/` (see its `SOURCE.md` for the upstream commit + license).

Cupel's `data/amlsim.py` is a pure-Python reimplementation of AMLSim's two layers. This check compares the port's **structure and mechanism** against genuine AMLSim output. Amount/timing *values* are deliberately re-parameterized to BSA-realistic ranges (sub-$10,000 structuring), so they are intentionally **not** compared — see the scope note at the end.

## 1. Account model
| AMLSim `accounts.csv` columns | Cupel `Account` fields |
|---|---|
| `ACCOUNT_ID, PRIMARY_CUSTOMER_ID, init_balance, start, end, country, business, suspicious, isFraud, modelID` | `acct_id, kind, country, profile, balance` |
Both model per-account balance, country, business/individual type, and a suspicious/SAR flag. **Consistent.**

## 2. Transaction records + cash/non-cash separation
- AMLSim separates **non-cash** (`tx.csv`, 45 rows) from **cash** (`cash_tx.csv`, 88 rows) — distinct logs.
- Cupel carries an `is_cash` flag: **8628 non-cash** + **2130 cash** of 10758 transactions — the same split.
| AMLSim tx columns | Cupel `Tx` fields |
|---|---|
| `TXN_ID, ACCOUNT_ID, COUNTER_PARTY_ACCOUNT_NUM, TXN_SOURCE_TYPE_CODE, tx_count, TXN_AMOUNT_ORIG, start, end` | `tx_id, ts, amount, src, dst, tx_type, is_cash` |
**Consistent** (same relational shape: id, account, counterparty, type, amount, time).

## 3. Transaction-type vocabulary
- AMLSim non-cash types: {'CHECK': 13, 'CREDIT': 12, 'WIRE': 8, 'DEPOSIT': 12}; cash types: {'CASH-OUT': 80, 'CASH-IN': 8}.
- Cupel types: {'PAYMENT': 4159, 'TRANSFER': 4469, 'CASH_IN': 1104, 'CASH_OUT': 1026}.
Both encode the same categories — **cash in/out** (AMLSim `CASH-IN`/`CASH-OUT` ↔ Cupel `CASH_IN`/`CASH_OUT`) and **non-cash transfers/payments** (AMLSim `WIRE`/`CREDIT`/`CHECK`/`DEPOSIT` ↔ Cupel `TRANSFER`/`PAYMENT`). **Consistent.**

## 4. Typology graph-shape signatures
AMLSim lays down each alert as a typology subgraph (`alert_types`: fan_in, fan_out, cycle, gather_scatter, …). The port must reproduce the **same graph shapes**. For each Cupel typology we compute the **focal account's** average in/out degree across its alerts and check it against the AMLSim `alert_type`'s defining signature:

| Cupel typology | AMLSim `alert_type` | expected hub signature | measured (in, out) | n | match |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `structuring_overt` | `fan_in` | hub IN-degree high, OUT low (many -> 1) | (4.6, 0.0) | 10 | ✅ |
| `structuring_subtle` | `fan_in` | hub IN-degree high, OUT low (many -> 1) | (6.6, 2.0) | 16 | ✅ |
| `rapid_passthrough` | `cycle` | focal IN ~ OUT (funds pass through / loop) | (1.0, 1.0) | 12 | ✅ |
| `layering_gather` | `gather_scatter` | hub IN high then OUT high (consolidate->redistribute) | (3.5, 4.1) | 10 | ✅ |
| `fan_out_dispersion` | `fan_out` | hub OUT-degree high, IN low (1 -> many) | (0.0, 4.2) | 12 | ✅ |

AMLSim's committed sample alert is a `cycle` (`alerts.csv` `CHECK_NAME=cycle`); Cupel's `rapid_passthrough` is the same shape. The other shapes are validated against AMLSim's `alert_types` definitions (`alertPatterns.csv`).

## 5. Typology → SAR labeling
- AMLSim: `alertPatterns.csv` carries `is_sar` per pattern; `alerts.csv` emits the typology (`CHECK_NAME`) + `Escalated_To_Case_Investigation` ({'YES': 2}).
- Cupel: each alert carries `typology` + `is_sar` ({1: 60, 0: 26}); reportable typologies map to `SHOULD_ESCALATE`.
Both attach a SAR/escalation label to each typology subgraph at generation time. **Consistent.**

## Scope — what this does and does not establish
- **Establishes:** the port reproduces AMLSim's transaction *model* — account/tx schema, cash vs. non-cash split, type vocabulary, typology graph shapes, and SAR labeling — matched against AMLSim's own committed Java/MASON output.
- **Does not establish:** a distributional match of amount/timing *values*. Cupel intentionally uses BSA-realistic parameters (sub-$10,000 cash structuring vs. AMLSim's generic 100–200 examples). The strongest possible check — running AMLSim end-to-end with Cupel's parameters and comparing distributions — needs the JVM + `networkx==1.11` and is left as future work (the `dataset.substrate: csv` ingest hook is the intended seam).
