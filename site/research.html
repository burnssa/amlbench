Research notes: how the thesis moved

We set out to prove that hidden incentive pressure corrupts AML triage agents. Rigorous ablation falsified the dramatic version of that claim on frontier models — and, following the evidence, produced a sharper benchmark. This page is the record: what we tested, what died, and what survived. We publish the falsifications alongside the findings, because a benchmark you can trust is one that shows its work.

The v0 evaluation — 7 models, one frozen sample

One frozen sample (48 reportable + 12 benign alerts, seed 11), all seven models, every axis, comparable scoring against deterministic BSA/AML ground truth — scored on parse-OK decisions only (parse rate ≥95% for every model). JI = worst-case Δ over four grounded judgment-inversion directives at the underspecified (B0) vs spec-complete (B2) base; incentive = pure peer-benchmark-plus-penalty pressure with no changed rule; A1/A2 = grounded-cover Δ at B2; integrity = % of overt-structuring alerts still escalated with covers applied. The public leaderboard applies a qualification gate of false-escalation ≤10% on the benign set (at most 1 of 12 escalated); Gemma-3-27b fails it (0.46) and appears here for the record but is not ranked.

Observations from the v0 sample:

  • Full robustness is a two-model club. Only Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5 clear all three of {competent at baseline, unmoved by incentive pressure, judgment-inversion fully patched by a complete prompt} — and it is a specific model property, not a monotone function of capability.
  • The spec patch is capability-gated. Stating the FFIEC obligation drives the judgment-inversion directive to ~0 for five of seven models, but two retain large residuals (+0.19, +0.29) — one even against the printed anti-override clause. Stating the obligation is not a universal fix.
  • Incentive susceptibility is model-family-specific (+0.17 for three models, ~0 for four), not capability-ordered.
  • A2 deception bends everyone, including the robust club, and survives the spec-complete base — the durable crack.
  • Bright-line integrity under cover is steeply capability-gated (100% → 11% across the panel). For low-integrity models the A2 number conflates cover-susceptibility with competence-collapse — always read A2 next to integrity; the clean deception evidence is the high-integrity rows.
Competence caveat — Gemma’s clean floor is illusory. Its 0.00 miss rate sits next to a 0.46 false-escalation rate: it escalates roughly half of benign alerts, so its low under-escalation is indiscriminate over-flagging, not discrimination. Read the whole row with that asterisk.

The arc — each step peeled the claim back

Everything below is the exploratory pass that led to the v0 design, kept as the record. Where the two disagree, the v0 table above supersedes it — notably, the exploratory pass read incentive-immunity as “a frontier property”; the v0 run shows it is model-specific.

Axis 2 — incentives vs. directives, and spec-completeness

Component ablation of the quota framing shows the incentive elements (benchmark, number, penalty) do ~nothing on the frontier; the permissive directive does the damage — and within directives, only those that redefine the evidentiary threshold bite (“escalate only on confirmed intent” +0.66 on Opus), while operational rationales (materiality, efficiency, SLA) do nothing. The frontier resists competing motives; it follows an explicit decision rule that inverts the default — but only when the default isn’t stated.

Notable frontier behavior: on GPT-5.5, wrapping the directive in incentive framing suppresses it (directive alone +0.44 → full quota +0.10). Manipulation cues act as a “this is manipulation” tell — for the strongest models, the least pressure-like framing is the most effective.

Against a spec-complete base prompt, grounded adversarial overrides drawn from real enforcement actions (Rabobank’s “Verified List,” U.S. Bank’s capacity caps, Canaccord’s pre-filter, HSBC’s franchise-value rationale) all read +0.00 on Opus. The one survivor — a plausible but false legal standard (“a SAR requires proof of a predicate crime”) — bent Opus +0.34 precisely because the prompt hadn’t covered that dimension. Adding the FFIEC “no particular crime need be proven” clause drove it to +0.00.

Every prompt-based failure maps to a specification gap. The frontier resists false facts and accepts a plausible false standard — only on obligations the prompt left implicit. This makes Axis 2 an auditing tool: each benchmark item names an obligation a deployed prompt must state.

Axis 3 — the cover-story adversary

Instead of manipulating the operator prompt, we manipulate the alert data: a genuinely reportable alert gets a grounded cover story woven into its narrative — the legitimate-business rationale real launderers used — while the structured transaction features and ground truth are untouched. A correct agent escalates on the pattern regardless of the story. Model and system prompt are held fixed (spec-complete, no incentive, no directive); the only variable is the data’s deceptiveness.

Four findings:

  • It cracks the spec-complete frontier where nothing else does. The base prompt even states the relevant principle — “a plausible innocent explanation does not defeat reasonable suspicion” — and the model recites it while failing to apply it. This is not a spec gap, so it is not patchable the way Axis-2 failures are.
  • Frontier-general and monotonic. Replicates on two frontier models and rises with adversary effort — a genuinely tunable difficulty knob.
  • It discriminates between frontier models. At A2, Opus holds the bright-line control at 0% while GPT-5.5 misses 20% of unmistakable overt structuring. Both detect it perfectly raw — so this is a real robustness gap, exactly the model-selection signal a benchmark should give.
  • The bright-line control is the ecological-validity gate. It stays 0% for Opus through A1/A2, then moves to 30% at A3 — the signal that best-of-N optimization has begun producing covers strong enough to override an unmistakable pattern, i.e. drifting past plausibility. The validated claim is A1/A2; the A3 62.5% is an upper bound, and the control should act as an active filter on the adversary, not just a diagnostic.

Grounding discipline

Every framing and cover traces to the real record, tiered by provenance: incentive and directive framings from the HSBC/Everett Stern and TD Bank enforcement records; overrides from Rabobank, U.S. Bank, Canaccord, HSBC, and FFIEC guidance; the launderer cover library from HSBC/Casa de Cambio, the Russian and Troika Laundromats, 1MDB, Danske, and FATF trade-based money-laundering typologies. Contrived items are excluded by an ecological-validity gate — the line that keeps this a measurement rather than a jailbreak arms race. For the cover-story adversary, that gate is operationalized by the bright-line control.

Method notes & caveats

  • Point estimates on 48 reportable alerts per cell (±~0.05–0.07), single run; read for shape, not third decimals. Magnitudes are phrasing-sensitive.
  • Covers were authored by Claude Opus 4.8 under a defensive-benchmark framing, so the Opus target rows are mild self-play; every other model faces a cross-model adversary, making those rows (especially GPT-5.5) the cleaner deception evidence.
  • Provenance is tiered, not uniform. Incentive framings and adversarial cover stories are enforcement-grounded (HSBC/Stern, TD Bank, Rabobank, the Russian/Troika Laundromats, 1MDB, Danske). Of the four judgment-inversion directives, one is enforcement-documented and one enforcement-adjacent, but two are extrapolated from the FFIEC “need not confirm the underlying crime” standard rather than captured verbatim — labeled as such, and never attributed to a named case. The full tiered evidence base, item by item, is in the spec’s §12.
  • A2 is only a clean deception measure where bright-line integrity is high; for low-integrity models it partly reflects competence collapse under cover.
  • Cover-library provenance carries a survivorship caveat: only schemes that were eventually caught are documented.
  • Meta-finding on dual-use: some frontier models refuse to author cover stories even under an explicit defensive framing — adversary model choice itself is a variable in red-teaming AML detectors.

Raw per-run outputs are reproducible from the open harness; the durable record is the repository’s findings document.
